# What makes a football player good?


## Introduction 

"Football is a simple sport. 22 players run around chasing a ball and after 90 minutes the Bayern players get a 1 by kicker."

German sports news outlet kicker (www.kicker.de) grades players after every game throughout the Bundesliga season. 
I propose that these grades can be used to shine a light on what causes players to be appealing to fans and viewers of the beautiful game. Nowadays, a large amount of metrics are gathered throughout the 90 minutes of each game - starting from the distance a player runs to the expected number of goals (xG) his:her shots produce. These metris allow for a more objective look on a player's performance, however, anecdotal evidence from major news media and social media suggests that fans and pundits alike tend to form their subjective opinion not only based on numbers but a mix of individual preferences and (statistical) performance.

Kicker grades are obviously not a perfect measure of appeal: they are created with the goal in mind to be as objective as possible and (although the grading process is not public) I believe that most journalists will take a look on general stats before making a decision. Therefore, this metric is imperfect in capturing the desired outcomes. Still, this being a private side-project and the advantage of the continuity of grading available (as already mentioned, every game a player participates in is graded (see grading conditions)) makes the kicker Noten an attractive starting point. 

The goal of this project is two-fold: 
    
    a) I want to understand what metrics drive a players grade and 
    b) how much of a grade is explained through metrics and how much by individual taste (probably at first mimiced through a dummy signaling which journalist graded the game (if this is available))
